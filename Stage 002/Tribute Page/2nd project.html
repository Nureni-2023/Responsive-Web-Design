Dr.Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug,third form the left,trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yield-part of his life-long war on hunger

Heres a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life

  • 1914-Born in Cresco, lowa
  • 1933-leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Deppression era program known as the "National Youth Adminstration"
  • 1935-Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Crops, helping starving America. "I saw how food changed them", he said."All of this left scars soon to me."
  • 1937-Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1941-Tiers to enroll in the millitary after the Pearl Harbor attack, but is rejected. Instead, the millitary asked his lab to work on waterproof glue, DDT to cony trol malaria,disinfectants, and other applied science.
  • 1942-Recieved a Ph.D. in Genetic and Plant Pathology.
  • 1944-Reject a 100% salaryincrease from Dupont, leave behind his pregnant wife,and file Mexico to head a new plant pathology program .Over the next 16 years ,his team breeds 6000 different strains of diseases resistant wheat including varieties for each respond well to fertilizer. It goes on to provide 95% of Mexico's wheat.
  • 1962-Visits Delhi and brings his high-yielding strains of wheat to the Indial subcontinent in time of help mitigate mass starvation due to rapidly expanding population
  • 1970-recieves the Noble Peace Prize
  • 1983-heps seven african countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields
  • 1984-becomes an dictinguished professor at A & M University
  • 2005-state"we will have to double the world food supply by 2050". Argues that genetically modified crops are the only way we can meet the demand, as we run out of arable land. says that GM crops are not inherently dangrous because "we've been genetically modified plants and animals for a long time. Long beforewe called it science, people were selecting the best breeds."
  • 2009-dies at the age of 95.
